Insights

Short notes on how to modernize platforms in regulated, asset-heavy environments — without losing governance.

A governance pattern for platform decisions

A simple executive pattern: outcomes, decision rights, controls, and “proof” artifacts.

  • Define 3–5 outcomes (speed, compliance, cost, reliability).
  • Assign decision rights (who decides what, when).
  • Make controls explicit (security, privacy, audit, architecture).
  • Require proof artifacts (HLD, risk assessment, KPI baselines).

Why “strategy → architecture → delivery” fails

Failure mode: strategy documents that don’t translate into governable work. Fix: connect roadmap items to explicit controls and measurable outcomes.

  • Each roadmap item must have an owner, control set, and success metric.
  • Architecture is the “contract” between intent and implementation.
  • Governance keeps the contract honest.

Resilience thinking for executives

In safety-critical environments, resilience is not a feature — it is an operating property of the whole system.

  • Design for degradation, not perfection.
  • Make dependencies visible (people, process, tech, suppliers).
  • Measure leading indicators (not only incidents).
